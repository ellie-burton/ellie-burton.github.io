About Me

Hi! I'm Ellie Burton, and I am a student at The University of Alabama studying Computer Science and Data Science.

I love engaging in academic experiences that allow me to explore the intersections of technology and impactful, real-life applications. I'm driven by a deep desire to use my technical skills to make a real difference, especially in fighting against sex trafficking and empowering education.

I love being faced with tough challenges, combining my analytical skills with creativity to find innovative solutions. I enjoy exploring complex ideas and working with diverse teams to achieve shared goals. To serve this goal, I am planning to pursue a Master's in Business Analytics at UA during my 5th year.

Academic Highlights

Sex Trafficking Analytics for Network Detection and Disruption

The STANDD initiative focuses on developing novel analytical techniques to group ad data from popular sites, creating tools that law enforcement can use to combat sex trafficking more effectively. By analyzing online sex advertisements using graph-based methods, we have built a dataset of over 60 million ads, scraping 100,000 daily, to identify potential trafficking situations.

Through this project, I have learned tremendously about data science and its real-world applications in making a societal impact. Our work demonstrates the power of technology in driving social change, with goals to expand our dataset and refine our algorithms to continue aiding in the fight against sex trafficking.

Economics Experiment Web Interface

My research focuses on understanding decision-making and risk. As a research assistant, my primary goal is to design and implement a dynamic web interface that enables researchers to conduct their experiments efficiently. This involves developing a full-stack application that integrates various tools and APIs for seamless data collection and analysis.

Through this role, I have gained valuable experience in software development and project management while contributing to innovative research that explores the complexities of human behavior and decision-making processes.

ACM-W Leadership

As the President of the UA Association of Computing Machinery - Women (ACM-W), I have revitalized the organization, growing membership from 4 to 45 active members. My leadership focuses on creating an inclusive environment where everyone feels valued and motivated.

I have organized workshops, guest lectures, and networking events that enhance learning and professional opportunities for women in computing. My goal is to foster teamwork, encourage individual growth, and provide resources that empower our members to excel in their academic and professional endeavors.
