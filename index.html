About Me

Hi! I'm Ellie Burton, and I am a student at The University of Alabama studying Computer Science and Data Science. I love learning new things and helping others.

Currently, I do research in the Institute of Data and Analytics at UA. I work on the Sex Trafficking Analytics for Network Detection and Disruption and Detection Initiative. This experience has shown me how data science and coding can translate into impactful, real-life applications. I'm driven by a deep desire to use my technical skills to make a real difference, especially in fighting against sex trafficking and empowering education.

I also serve as the president of UA’s Association of Computing Machinery Women’s chapter, where I have restarted and officially certified the club, as well as increased membership by 150%. I strive to create spaces where everyone feels valued, fostering teamwork and encouraging individual growth.

Overall, I thrive when faced with tough challenges, combining my analytical skills with creativity to find innovative solutions. I enjoy translating complex ideas and working with diverse teams to achieve shared goals. To serve this goal, I am planning to pursue a Master's in Business Analytics at UA.
